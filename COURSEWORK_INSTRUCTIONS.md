Every student is required to complete a final project related to Large Language Models (LLMs) and present it during the final classes using a poster.

### 1) Submission Requirements

- **Deadline:** Submit the project report via the Final Project Google Form before **May 1st, 2026**.
- **File naming:** `Last Name, First Name.pdf`
- **Format:** Submissions must follow the **2025 ACL long paper template**.
- **Examples:** High-quality examples are available in the **ACL 2024 Project Reports archive**.

### 4) Future Benefits

Poster participation is optional and does not affect your grade.  
However, active and high-quality participation is highly valued by Yiwei Wang and may support future recommendation letters.

### 5) Final Project Evaluation Criteria

Evaluation focuses on two major NLP conference dimensions: **Soundness** and **Excitement**.

#### 5.1 Soundness (Technical Integrity and Rigor)

Soundness checks whether findings are trustworthy and scientifically valid.

- **Experimental rigor**
  - Did you use appropriate baselines (for example, standard zero-shot or few-shot prompts)?
  - Is the data size large enough to support your claims?
- **Ablation studies**
  - If you propose a multi-step method, did you remove components to verify each one adds value?
- **Error analysis**
  - Strong reports do not only show metrics; they explain why failures happen using qualitative analysis of incorrect samples.
- **Metric choice**
  - Are your metrics (for example, Accuracy, F1, ROUGE, Human Eval) aligned with the task goal?

#### 5.2 Excitement (Innovation and Impact)

Excitement checks novelty and how interesting the work is.

- **Originality**
  - Does the project go beyond simple plug-and-play?
  - Strong projects offer new insights into LLM behavior, prompting strategies, or unique applications.
- **Clarity of communication**
  - Is the report clearly written using the ACL LaTeX template?
  - Are figures and tables intuitive and professional?
- **Task difficulty**
  - Challenging under-explored problems (for example, domain reasoning or efficiency optimization) are more exciting than repeating common benchmarks.

**Instructor's note:** Active poster engagement that shows soundness or excitement may help with future recommendation letters, but it does not affect course grading.
